danes in bali

around eight years ago, nadia sara boan sørensen and jeppe bech-sørensen began an adventurous journey in southeast asia. their story started with a chance meeting in spain that led to a shared dream of moving to asia and creating something together. they explored various locations, but ultimately fell in love with bali for its rich culture and warmth.

Three cafes

with "cøpenhagen" their first café, they brewed more than just coffee; they cultivated a community where friendships flourished and dreams soared. their café's success led to two more, each embodying their vision of balinese charm infused with scandinavian simplicity.

nordic traditions

nordic food traditions feature preserved foods like smoked fish, pickled vegetables, and cured meats, with staples such as potatoes and rye bread.

danish traditions

danish eating traditions are rich and diverse, featuring beloved treats and festive meals. kanelsnegle (cinnamon rolls) are enjoyed year-round. æbleskiver, spherical pancakes with powdered sugar and jam, are popular during christmas. julefrokost, a christmas lunch, includes pickled herring, liver pâté, and meatballs. påskefrokost, an easter lunch, features similar dishes. these traditions celebrate good food, company, and cultural heritage.
